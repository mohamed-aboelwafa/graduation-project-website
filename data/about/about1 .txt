As one of the leading higher institutes in the Arab Republic of Egypt, Abu Qir Higher Institutes were established in 1998 in line with the Egyptian government's policy to encourage private education. The Supreme Council of Egyptian Universities and Private Institutes issued its license, which has been valid since the academic year 2000/2001.


The institutes accept students nominated through the coordination system of General Secondary Education (Thanaweya Amma), Al-Azhar, technical diploma holders, and those who missed coordination for up to one year, as well as transfer students from other colleges and institutes. Admission is subject to the coordination regulations set by the Ministry of Higher Education. According to the equivalency decision, graduates of the institutes are eligible to register with the relevant professional syndicates, enroll in postgraduate studies at Egyptian and foreign universities, and apply to military colleges for specialized officers.


The institutes are distinguished by a wide range of outstanding physical and human resources, including computer labs, language labs, lecture halls equipped with the latest educational technologies, a training kitchen, a training hotel, a training restaurant, educational travel agencies, an educational museum, antiquities restoration labs, an art studio, a football field, basketball court, volleyball court, five-a-side football field, gymnasium, cafeterias, libraries, data show halls, electronics labs, and hardware labs.
